Discover the World of Bees

Bees are flying insects known for their role in pollination and producing honey. They are part of the insect order Hymenoptera and are found all over the world.

Basic Bee Facts

Bees come in thousands of species, including honeybees, bumblebees, and solitary bees. They have hairy bodies that help collect pollen, and most species live in colonies with queens, workers, and drones.

A bee's life revolves around gathering nectar and pollen from flowers, which they use for food and to make honey (in some species). They are essential to many ecosystems.
